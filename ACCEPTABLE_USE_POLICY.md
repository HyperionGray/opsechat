## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs your use of the opsechat service ("Service"), a privacy-focused anonymous communication platform. This AUP is designed to protect our users, the Service, and the broader internet community from illegal, abusive, or harmful activities.

By using opsechat, you agree to comply with this AUP. Violations may result in immediate suspension or termination of access, and we will cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations of illegal activities.

---

## 2. Permitted Use

opsechat is designed for **legitimate privacy-preserving communications**, including but not limited to:

✅ **Permitted Activities:**
- Secure, private communication with trusted parties
- Protection of sensitive business communications
- Whistleblowing and journalism activities
- Personal privacy protection
- Security research and testing (with appropriate authorization)
- Avoiding surveillance in oppressive regimes
- General private correspondence

## 3. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are **strictly prohibited** when using opsechat:

### 3.1 Illegal Activities
❌ Any activity that violates local, state, national, or international law
❌ Distribution or possession of illegal content (including child exploitation material)
❌ Drug trafficking or illegal substance distribution
❌ Human trafficking or exploitation
❌ Terrorism, violent extremism, or threats of violence
❌ Money laundering or financial fraud
❌ Identity theft or impersonation
❌ Stalking, harassment, or threatening communications

### 3.2 Abuse and Harassment
❌ Harassment, bullying, or threatening behavior
❌ Hate speech or promotion of violence against individuals or groups
❌ Doxxing (publishing private information without consent)
❌ Coordinated harassment campaigns
❌ Sexual harassment or unwanted sexual content

### 3.3 Malicious Technical Activities
❌ Distribution of malware, viruses, or malicious code
❌ Phishing attempts or social engineering attacks (except authorized security testing)
❌ Network attacks, DDoS, or unauthorized system access
❌ Exploiting vulnerabilities in the Service or other systems
❌ Bypassing security measures or access controls
❌ Spamming or unsolicited bulk messaging

### 3.4 Intellectual Property Violations
❌ Distribution of copyrighted material without authorization
❌ Piracy or illegal file sharing
❌ Trademark infringement
❌ Distribution of stolen proprietary information

### 3.5 Fraud and Deception
❌ Scams, fraud, or deceptive practices
❌ Pyramid schemes or multi-level marketing fraud
❌ Phishing for credentials or sensitive information
❌ Fraudulent investment schemes

### 3.6 Abuse of Service
❌ Automated scraping or data harvesting
❌ Excessive use that degrades service for others
❌ Creating multiple accounts to evade restrictions
❌ Reselling or redistributing the Service without authorization
❌ Using the Service to violate another service's terms

## 4. Content Restrictions

### 4.1 File Types
The Service currently supports **text-based communications only**. When attachments are implemented:
- ✅ **Allowed:** Text files, documents, source code
- ❌ **Prohibited:** Videos, images (initially), executable files

### 4.2 Content Moderation
While we are committed to privacy, we reserve the right to:
- Implement automated spam filtering
- Block known malicious domains and IPs
- Terminate accounts engaged in abuse
- Report illegal activity to appropriate authorities

## 5. Law Enforcement Cooperation

**We take illegal activity seriously.** 

Despite our commitment to privacy and anonymity:
- We **will cooperate fully** with law enforcement investigations
- We **will comply** with valid legal process (warrants, subpoenas, court orders)
- We **will provide** any data or metadata we have access to when legally required
- We **will not** protect users engaged in illegal activities

**Important Notes:**
- Due to our zero-disk-touching architecture, we have limited data retention
- Communications are ephemeral and may not be available after expiry
- We do not have access to encrypted content (by design)
- Metadata may be limited due to Tor anonymity
- We will provide whatever information is technically available and legally required

Anyone using this Service for **nefarious purposes will face the full consequences of their actions**, and Hyperion Gray will assist law enforcement to the best of our technical ability.

### 6.1 Burner Email System
The burner email system is for **legitimate temporary email needs only**:
- ✅ Temporary account verification
- ✅ Avoiding spam on personal email
- ✅ Testing email systems
- ❌ **NOT** for spamming others
- ❌ **NOT** for evading blocks or bans
- ❌ **NOT** for mass unsolicited messaging

### 6.2 Rate Limits
We implement rate limiting to prevent abuse:
- Burner email generation is rate-limited
- Message sending is rate-limited
- Excessive use will result in temporary or permanent restrictions

### 6.3 Spam Filtering
- Incoming emails are subject to spam filtering
- Known spam sources are blocked
- Abuse reports are investigated

## 8. Privacy and Data Retention

### 8.1 Our Commitments
- We do **not** store your communications on disk (zero-disk policy)
- We do **not** have access to your encrypted content
- We do **not** log your IP address (Tor anonymity)
- We do **not** retain persistent user profiles

### 8.2 Data We May Have
Despite our privacy-preserving design, we may have:
- Session identifiers (temporary, random)
- Timestamps of service usage
- Metadata about service access
- Technical logs for debugging (if enabled)

### 8.3 Data Retention
- Chat messages: Deleted after 3 minutes
- Burner emails: Expired after 24 hours (default)
- Email content: Stored in memory only, cleared on session end
- Logs: Minimal, auto-deleted after 30 days (if any)
